# Studio Monitor Setup Guide: Near-Field Optimization

## 1. Core Geometric Principles
The foundation of an accurate monitoring environment is the **equilateral triangle**. Prioritize this above all other factors.

*   **The Rule:** The distance between the monitors must equal the distance from each monitor's acoustic center to your ears.
*   **The Angle:** This naturally creates a 60° toe-in. 
*   **The Alignment:** Aim the monitors so their acoustic axes (the line from the tweeter) intersect at a point slightly behind your head (approx. 10–15 cm). This widens the effective "sweet spot" and improves stereo stability.

## 2. Positioning Hierarchy
If space constraints force a compromise, follow this priority list:

1.  **Priority 1: Geometry (The Triangle).** Never compromise the equilateral triangle. Phase coherence is non-negotiable for mixing.
2.  **Priority 2: Symmetry.** Ensure left and right monitors are equidistant from their respective side walls to prevent asymmetrical reflections and comb filtering.
3.  **Priority 3: Boundary Distance (The Wall).** Aim for at least 20–40 cm from the rear wall. If you must move them closer to maintain the triangle, do so—boundary-induced low-end buildup is more easily managed than phase smear.

## 3. Near-Field Execution Checklist
- [ ] **Floor Stands:** Place monitors on floor stands, not the desk surface, to eliminate cabinet vibration and desk-bounce reflections.
- [ ] **Decoupling:** Use isolation pads (Sorbothane, MoPADs, or rubber gaskets) between the monitor and the stand plate.
- [ ] **Height:** Align the acoustic center (the space between tweeter and woofer) with your ears at the seated mix position.
- [ ] **Wall Clearance:** Maintain >20 cm gap from the rear wall. Avoid corners where distance to side and rear walls is identical.
- [ ] **Phase Integrity:** Use a Correlation Meter on the master bus. Playing mono noise should result in a reading at or near +1.

## 4. Troubleshooting & Calibration
If the sound is compromised, address the root cause:

| Symptom | Potential Cause | Fix |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **"Hollow" Stereo Center** | Speakers too far apart | Reduce width; maintain triangle. |
| **"Muddy" Low-End** | Wall proximity/SBIR | Adjust Room Control switches; add absorption behind monitors. |
| **High-Freq Roll-off** | Off-axis listening | Re-adjust toe-in to target ears. |
| **Phase Smear** | Desk/Floor reflections | Ensure stands are decoupled and solid. |

## 5. Verification Workflow
1. **Pink Noise Test:** Listen for a stable, focused phantom center.
2. **Head Sway:** Move your head slightly; the image should remain consistent.
3. **Correlation Check:** Confirm phase coherence on your master bus.
