# Acoustic Tracking Master Guide

## I. Flagship Setup: 4x6m Master Bedroom
**Objective:** Maximum acoustic control and dry, production-grade signal acquisition.

### Geometry
- **Primary Anchor (Front):** Upright mattress against the wall furthest from the window. Face this wall while playing.
- **The Shield (Rear):** Backdrop stand with heavy comforter placed between the performer and the window.
- **Lateral Control:** Headboard (5" fabric) positioned to one side. Apply a blanket to the opposite wall if "flutter echo" (metallic ringing) persists.

### Mic Configuration
- **Guitar (Stereo):** ZPC-1s in **ORTF** (17cm capsule distance, 110° angle).
- **Vocals (Mono):** MXL 770 on a wide-spread leg camera tripod, 15–20cm from mouth.

---

## II. Utility Setup: Living Room
**Objective:** "Vibrant" recording sessions where the room's character serves the performance.

### Geometry
- **Window Mitigation:** Backdrop stand + comforter is **mandatory**.
- **Diffusion Anchor:** Face the mic toward the sofa/guitars wall to leverage them as natural diffusers.
- **Containment:** Keep the laundry room door **closed** to prevent low-frequency resonance.

### Mic Configuration
- **Guitar (Stereo):** ZPC-1s in **XY** (0cm/touching, 90° angle). Mandatory for phase coherence.

---

## III. Production Workflow (SOP)
| Stage | Task | Objective |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. Prep** | Click Track + Guide Vocal | Establish tempo and phrasing. |
| **2. Tracking** | Acoustic Guitar | Lay foundation using the **Stereo Pair**. |
| **3. Comping** | Edit | Surgical timing and alignment. |
| **4. Overdub** | Vocals | Swap inputs at SSL 2+; record with **MXL 770**. |
| **5. Mixing** | Balance | Use HD 490 Pros for critical listening. |

---

## IV. Hardware & Interface Management (SSL 2+)
- **Strain Relief:** Secure cables to the desk edge to protect ports from physical stress during daily swaps.
- **Isolation:** Physically unplug inactive mics to prevent room bleed and phase artifacts.

---

## V. Universal Calibration
- **The Clap Test:** Perform a sharp, loud clap at the mic position before every session. If a metallic "zing" or "flutter" is heard, identify the reflective surface (glass/bare wall) and cover it with fabric until the decay is dead.
- **Tripod Stability:** Always use max leg angle spread for the vocal tripod. Loop the XLR cable over a tripod leg for stress relief.

---

## VI. Strategic Summary
1. **Prioritize the Bedroom:** Treat the 4x6m room as your "blank canvas." Total control is superior to baked-in room character.
2. **Embrace Asymmetry:** Use room furniture to diffuse sound naturally.
3. **The Golden Rule:** Face your most absorptive surface (mattress) and shield your most reflective surface (window).
